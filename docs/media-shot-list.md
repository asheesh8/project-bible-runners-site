# VillageServer — Media & Video Shot List

**For:** Doug / Children Evangelism Fellowship field teams
**From:** ArkiTech-Sol (VillageServer site team)
**Prepared:** July 1, 2026 — for the July 5 conversation
**Why this exists:** Real field photography and video will do more for the site than
anything AI-generated. We don't need expensive cinematic footage — we need *authentic,
well-lit, real* shots of the equipment and the people using it. A clear phone photo of a
real village showing beats a polished AI render every time. This list tells the field
teams exactly what to capture so we can drop it straight into the website.

---

## Capture guidelines (please share with whoever shoots)

- **Real over pretty.** Genuine moments — someone actually reading Scripture on a phone,
  a real projector showing in a real village — matter more than staged "cinematic" shots.
- **Shoot horizontal (landscape) for the website.** Most site slots are wide. Vertical is
  fine as a bonus for social, but landscape is the priority.
- **Highest resolution the device allows.** Modern phone photos are perfect. Don't zoom
  digitally — walk closer instead.
- **Good light.** Shoot in daylight or well-lit rooms. Avoid heavy backlight (bright
  window behind the subject). Golden hour (early morning / late afternoon) looks best
  outdoors.
- **Hands and faces tell the story.** Close-ups of hands holding a card/phone, and faces
  reacting to what they're watching, are the most valuable shots we can get.
- **Consent & safety first.** Get verbal permission before photographing people,
  especially children. In sensitive regions, avoid faces that could put anyone at risk —
  shoot over-the-shoulder, hands only, or silhouettes instead. Flag any media that must
  **not** be published publicly.
- **Label as you go.** A one-line note per shot ("projector showing, Uganda, March") saves
  hours later.

## Priority 1 — Must have (these fill the biggest gaps on the site)

### The five reach tiers (front-page "How the reach multiplies" section)
One clear photo of **each** step, ideally in real field use:

1. **microSD card** — a hand holding the card, or the card going into a phone. Close-up.
2. **Wi-Fi sharing hub** — the pocket hub with a few phones nearby connecting to it.
3. **Raspberry Pi VillageServer** — the case powered on, a small group of phones around it.
4. **Projector & audio** — a projector showing a gospel film to a crowd at night/dusk.
5. **Satellite** — the dish/LNB setup outdoors, ideally with a person aligning it.

### People actually using it
- Someone **reading Scripture on a phone** (over-the-shoulder is great, and safer).
- **Phone-to-phone sharing** — one person handing/beaming a file to another.
- A **group gathered watching** a film or listening to audio together.
- A **local leader teaching someone else** to use it (this is the whole point — reproducibility).

---

## Priority 2 — Strongly wanted

### Testimonials (Testimonials page)
- **Short video clips (30–90 sec)** of field workers or believers sharing, in their own
  words, what receiving the Word offline meant. Even phone-shot, close, with clear audio.
- A **portrait photo** of each person who gives a testimony (with consent), for the card.

### Team / setup ("Field test run" section)
- The team **assembling a kit** on a table (phones, case, cables laid out).
- **Solar panel + battery** powering the kit outdoors.
- **Workshop / prep** shots — building or testing before deployment.
- These can update the current test-run photos with real field versions.

### Partners / context (Ministry Partners / About)
- Any photos with **CEF / partner ministries** on the ground (with their permission).
- Wide **establishing shots** of a village, church, or gathering that set the scene.

---

## Priority 3 — Nice to have

- **Solar / power** close-ups (panel, battery pack, cabling in use).
- **Printed materials** being handed out or used in the field.
- **Maps / travel** — getting to remote locations (roads, boats, footpaths).
- **Detail texture shots** — the case, ports, screens — for section backgrounds.
- **Night scenes** — a projector glowing in the dark is a powerful hero-type image.

## Video b-roll wish list (for future edits / hero background)

Short, steady clips — 5–15 seconds each, landscape, no zooming:

- Card sliding into a phone; screen lighting up.
- Phones connecting to the hub / Pi (screens showing the library).
- A crowd watching a projected film (from behind the crowd, faces lit by the screen).
- Satellite dish against the sky; hands adjusting it.
- Hands turning through Scripture on a phone.
- A local person smiling / reacting to what they're seeing.

## Technical specs (so it drops straight into the site)

| Use | Orientation | Ideal size | Notes |
|-----|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Photo cards / section images | Landscape | ≥ 1600 px wide | Will be cropped to fill; keep the subject centered |
| Testimonial portraits | Square or landscape | ≥ 1000 px | Face framed with a little headroom |
| Video (testimony / b-roll) | Landscape 16:9 | 1080p is plenty | Clear audio matters more than 4K |
| Hero background (optional) | Landscape 16:9 | 1080p+ | Slow, steady motion; nothing frantic |

**Formats:** JPG/PNG for photos, MP4 (H.264) for video. Send originals — we'll compress
and crop on our side. A shared Google Drive / Dropbox folder is the easiest handoff.
