# VillageServer Initiative — Vermont nonprofit readiness

Status: preparation checklist only. Nothing in this file means that VillageServer Initiative has been incorporated, recognized by the IRS, or authorized to solicit charitable gifts.

## Vermont formation

- [ ] Search the [Vermont business registry](https://bizfilings.vermont.gov/online/BusinessInquire/) and confirm an available legal name.
- [ ] Choose the incorporator, initial directors, officers, and a Vermont registered agent.
- [ ] Confirm a Vermont registered-office street address and the registered agent's email.
- [ ] Draft articles identifying the corporation as a public-benefit corporation and include appropriate charitable purpose and dissolution language.
- [ ] Have the required incorporator or directors sign and file the articles through the Vermont Secretary of State portal.
- [ ] Budget the current **$155** Vermont nonprofit articles filing fee.
- [ ] Adopt bylaws, conflict-of-interest policy, initial board resolutions, fiscal year, and recordkeeping process.

Vermont's required articles content is in [11B V.S.A. § 2.02](https://legislature.vermont.gov/statutes/fullchapter/11B/002). The current filing fee is in [11B V.S.A. § 1.22](https://legislature.vermont.gov/statutes/section/11B/001/00001.22).

## Federal and operating steps

- [ ] After state formation, obtain a free EIN directly from the [IRS](https://www.irs.gov/ein).
- [ ] Open an organization bank account and establish bookkeeping and gift-receipt controls.
- [ ] Determine eligibility for Form 1023-EZ or prepare Form 1023 using the [IRS exemption application process](https://www.irs.gov/charities-non-profits/exempt-organization-exemption-application).
- [ ] Do not represent gifts as tax-deductible until the organization's status and any fiscal-sponsor arrangement are verified.
- [ ] Review Vermont charitable-solicitation, annual-report, federal-return, and every-state fundraising requirements with qualified counsel or a nonprofit professional.

This is an implementation checklist, not legal or tax advice. A Vermont nonprofit attorney or experienced formation service should review the articles, bylaws, and fundraising plan before filing.
