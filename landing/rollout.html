Board brief - proof of concept

Rollout and Reach

Rollout should begin with small pilots, clear training, honest feedback, and simple reach reporting.

Plain answer

A good rollout proves that local people can use the system without the original builder in the room.

Field steps

Use it this way

  1. Choose a pilot siteStart where a trusted partner can observe real use and report honestly.
  2. Train one local leadMake sure one person can unpack, power, connect, open, and share.
  3. Use the PDF handoutsLeave printed and digital instructions with the kit.
  4. Measure simple reachTrack approximate people trained, devices connected, files shared, and groups served.
  5. Record blockersWrite down power, language, device, content, and training issues.
  6. Improve before scalingDo not multiply kits until the pilot process is understandable.

Proof of concept

A small test that proves the process works in real hands.

Training measure

Can a local lead repeat it without guessing?

Reach measure

People trained, devices reached, groups served, and resources shared.

Board takeaway

Reach is credible when it is reported simply and honestly.
