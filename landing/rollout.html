Proof of concept

Measure behavior, not hype.

Before pilots report results, the site should describe targets and capacities honestly.

01

Devices connected

How many phones or tablets successfully join and open the local library.

02

Resources saved

How often users save Scripture, audio, videos, or PDFs for later offline use.

03

Group sessions

How projector sessions, trainings, or listening groups are held and repeated.

04

Local leaders trained

Whether local leaders can operate the kit without the original technical team.

05

Power reliability

Whether solar, battery, or local power keeps the system stable long enough.

06

Support issues

What breaks, what confuses people, and what needs clearer labeling.

Honest language

Do not claim outcomes before they exist.

  • Say plannedUse “planned pilot regions” until pilots are complete.
  • Say targetUse “target partner count” before agreements are signed.
  • Say capacityUse “supports up to” only when the hardware plan backs it.
  • Say measuredUse “reached” only when there is field evidence.
