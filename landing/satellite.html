Optional connection

Satellite adds a live link. It does not replace the local library.

Satellite can help with updates, limited live access, field reporting, or remote support. VillageServer should still work locally when the sky is blocked, power is low, or service is unavailable.

Setup pattern

Keep the satellite path separate and obvious.

Find open sky

Place the dish where trees, buildings, mountains, or vehicles do not block the view.

Power carefully

Satellite gear can draw more power than the local server. Confirm the battery and solar plan first.

Connect only when needed

Use satellite for update windows or support, then let the local library serve users offline.

Write down results

Record whether signal, speed, and power were good enough for that location.

Important limits

Satellite is powerful, but it is not magic.

  • Sky mattersObstructions can break the connection even when the kit is healthy.
  • Power mattersPlan battery and solar capacity before promising long sessions.
  • Service variesProvider availability, plan terms, and performance differ by country.
  • Offline remains firstThe library should remain usable with satellite turned off.

Set up the dish

Assemble, connect, and aim the reception system.

A reception system is a chain: dish → LNB → receiver → VillageServer. Get each link right, aim at open sky, and lock the signal. Step through it below.

  1. 1Open sky
  2. 2Assemble
  3. 3Connect
  4. 4Aim
  5. 5Lock signal
  6. 6Capture

1 · Find open sky

Place the dish where trees, buildings, mountains, or vehicles do not block the view toward the satellite. A clear line of sight matters more than anything else.

2 · Assemble the dish

Mount the dish solidly. A segmented flyaway dish — an older dish divided into sections that fit a suitcase — is reassembled on site to recreate the original dish surface for easier transport.

3 · Connect the chain

Fix the LNB at the dish’s focal point, run coax to the satellite receiver, then connect the receiver to a monitor or the VillageServer. Confirm a stable power source → — satellite gear can draw more than the server.

4 · Aim the dish

Set elevation and azimuth for your location. Use a satellite finder app, signal meter, compass, and inclinometer to point accurately. Move slowly — small changes make big differences.

5 · Lock the signal

Watch the receiver’s signal-quality display and fine-tune until it peaks, then tighten every bolt so wind cannot shift the aim.

6 · Capture & store

Receive the content, then store it on the VillageServer for offline sharing. The satellite receives the signal, the VillageServer stores it, and the people share the message. Build the library →
