Satellite Systems and Content Acquisition

A bridge, not the foundation

Satellite is the optional part of the VillageServer kit. Useful for remote content downloads and field coordination — but the local library should work completely offline before satellite ever enters the plan.

Satellite is helpful in the right setting — a team working days from the nearest town, needing to pull down fresh content or send field updates. But it's a bridge, not the road. VillageServer is strongest when the local library is already working offline.

When satellite actually helps

Most VillageServer deployments don't need satellite at all. The library is loaded before the team leaves, and everything runs offline in the field. Satellite becomes useful in specific situations:

  • Remote content updates — downloading a new language version or updated resource pack when too far from cellular data
  • Field reports — sending observations, reach numbers, or testimonials back to base during extended deployments
  • New resource acquisition — pulling a specific file or library that wasn't included in the original kit
  • Emergency communication — when no other connectivity is available and contact with the team is necessary

Setting it up in the field

  1. Confirm satellite is actually needed

    If cellular data is available — even slow 3G — use it instead. Satellite adds cost, complexity, and setup time that isn't worth it when a hotspot works.

  2. Find open sky

    The dish needs an unobstructed line to the satellite. Even a single tree branch can break the link. Scout the location before setting up.

  3. Set up on stable ground

    The dish must stay aligned during use. Wind, soft ground, or accidental movement will drop the connection mid-download.

  4. Power it properly

    Satellite hardware draws more current than the Pi or projector. Plan this separately in your battery and solar budget.

  5. Download only what the mission needs

    Avoid pulling large files out of habit. Every unnecessary download wastes battery, data allowance, and time.

  6. Test the local library offline after

    Disconnect the satellite and confirm all core content still works without it. The session should never depend on the satellite link staying active.

Build the offline library first. Satellite is a bridge for updates — not the heart of the system.
