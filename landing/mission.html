Board brief - why this exists

Mission Statement

VillageServer helps missionaries and local leaders carry Scripture, gospel media, and practical ministry resources into places where internet, electricity, and local-language teaching are limited.

Plain answer

The mission is simple: make gospel access feel obvious for the person holding the phone. A person should be able to connect, open, listen, read, save, and share without needing a technical background.

Field steps

Use it this way

  1. Listen firstAsk the missionary or local leader what language, device type, and power reality they face.
  2. Choose the simplest pathStart with the lightest access method: phone sharing, microSD, USB, local Wi-Fi, projector, or satellite only when needed.
  3. Load trusted resourcesPrepare Scripture, audio, films, children's resources, and training files in a clear folder structure.
  4. Teach one repeatable actionShow one person how to connect, save one resource, and share that same resource with someone nearby.
  5. Report what workedWrite down what people used, what confused them, and what should change before the next rollout.

Who it serves

Missionaries, churches, schools, clinics, and trusted local leaders working in low-connectivity communities.

What it carries

Bible text, audio Scripture, gospel films, children's resources, discipleship files, and local-language training.

What it avoids

No complicated login, no dependence on mobile data, and no assumption that internet will be available.

Board takeaway

VillageServer is not a gadget project. It is a practical gospel-access tool for places ordinary websites cannot reach.
