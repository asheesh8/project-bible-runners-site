Board brief - one-page overview

Full Initiative Overview

VillageServer combines offline libraries, phone sharing, local Wi-Fi, power, projection, and optional satellite into a single field model for gospel access.

Plain answer

VillageServer is a portable gospel technology project. It combines an offline library, local Wi-Fi, phone sharing, power, projection, and optional satellite into a simple field model.

Field steps

Use it this way

  1. Start with the missionExplain that the goal is gospel access in places where normal internet resources do not reach well.
  2. Identify the field settingAsk about language, power, device types, group size, travel distance, and whether live internet is realistic.
  3. Choose the kit levelMatch the need to a phone-sharing path, Raspberry Pi library, power kit, projector setup, or satellite-enabled kit.
  4. Prepare training materialPrint the correct PDF handout and keep a phone-friendly version on the kit.
  5. Pilot before scalingTest with a small group, write down blockers, and improve the kit before sending more.

Mission

Bring Scripture and Christian resources into communities with limited internet, power, and teaching access.

Technology

Use a Raspberry Pi local server so nearby phones can open the library without mobile data.

Field use

Help people read, listen, watch, save, and share resources on phones they already understand.

Rollout

Grow through pilots, trusted partners, honest reporting, and clear training materials.
