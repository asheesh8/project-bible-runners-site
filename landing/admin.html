📋  Campaigns

What is a campaign?

A fundraising run — like Spring Uganda Run 2026. Each campaign gets its own page on the site (/spring-uganda-run), its own donation goal, and its own Zeffy link. Think of it as a dedicated fundraiser with a start and end date.

What is the slug?

The URL identifier. Whatever you put in Slug becomes the page address. Example: slug spring-uganda-2026 → visitors go to yoursite.com/spring-uganda-2026. Use lowercase letters and dashes only — no spaces.

How do I update how much money has been raised?

Click Edit on the campaign → change Raised so far ($) → Save. The progress bar on the campaign page updates automatically. Do this after each Zeffy payout or whenever you check your total.

What does "Active" mean?

The active campaign is the featured one shown on the home page campaign card. Only one campaign should be active at a time. When a run ends, uncheck Active on the old one and check it on the new one.

What is the Zeffy URL?

The donation form link from zeffy.com for that specific campaign. Log into Zeffy → open the fundraiser → copy the URL from your browser. Paste it here. Every "Give" button on that campaign page will point to it.

How do I start a new campaign?

1. Create the Zeffy fundraiser first and copy its URL.
2. Click New campaign here → fill in name, slug, story, goal, and Zeffy URL.
3. Check Mark as active when you're ready to feature it.
4. The campaign page goes live at /[your-slug] immediately.

📝  Blog & Updates

What are posts for?

Field updates, testimonies, prayer requests, stories from the run. They appear in the Updates section on campaign pages. Use them to keep donors informed and excited — even a short "we arrived safely in Uganda" post goes a long way.

What's the difference between Draft and Published?

Draft — saved but invisible on the site. Good for writing ahead of time.
Published — live on the site immediately. Check the Publish immediately box when you're ready. You can unpublish any time by editing and unchecking it.

Can I write posts from the field on a phone?

Yes — the admin works on mobile. Go to yoursite.com/admin in your phone browser. The sidebar collapses on small screens; tap Blog & Updates to get to posts. Keep the body short for slow connections.

🖼  Photos

How do I add a photo?

Photos are added by URL — not file upload. Upload your photo to Google Drive, Dropbox, or any image host, get a direct link, paste it here. Google Drive tip: share the file publicly → right-click → Copy link.

Which category should I use?

Field — general: anything from the field that doesn't fit below.
Field — distribution: photos of Bibles being handed out.
Field — prayer: prayer circles, worship moments.
VillageServer kit: the solar case, Raspberry Pi, router setup.
Team: runners, staff, group shots.
Other: events, churches, donors.

⚙️  General

How fast do changes appear on the site?

Instantly. The site fetches live from Supabase on every page load — there's no cache to clear, no deploy to trigger. Save here, open the site in a new tab, it's there.

What if I make a mistake?

Just edit the item and save again. Nothing is permanent until you click Delete — and even then there's a confirmation popup. If you accidentally delete something important, it can be restored directly in the Supabase table editor at supabase.com.

Someone else needs access — can I share the password?

Yes, but everyone shares the same password (ADMIN_PASSWORD in Vercel). There's no per-user login. To change the password, update the env var in Vercel and redeploy. Let anyone who had the old password know.

How do I get to this page without knowing the URL?

Scroll to the very bottom of the main site → find Built by ArkiTech-Sol in the footer → click it 3 times quickly. That's the hidden shortcut. Don't share it publicly.
