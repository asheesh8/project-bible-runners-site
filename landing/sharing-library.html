Sharing the Library

The library becomes powerful when one person can save a useful resource and pass it to someone nearby.

Plain answer

The goal is not only access. The goal is repeatable sharing: one person receives, understands, saves, and passes the resource forward.

Field steps

Use it this way

  1. Connect or copyUse VillageServer Wi-Fi, microSD, USB, or phone sharing to get one resource onto the first device.
  2. Choose one useful filePick the file most likely to help immediately: Scripture, audio, a film, or a training PDF.
  3. Save it clearlyPut it in a folder or app the person can find again.
  4. Share to one personUse the simplest nearby transfer method for the two phones.
  5. Ask the new person to repeatHave the receiver share it again so the method becomes local knowledge.
  6. Leave printed stepsUse the handout so sharing continues after the team leaves.

What to share first

One file that is clearly useful, not the whole library.

Who should learn

A local person who is patient and likely to teach others.

What to avoid

Complicated folder names, too many files, and unexplained apps.

Board takeaway

Sharing turns a kit into a multiplying access path.
