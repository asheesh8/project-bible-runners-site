Raspberry Pi VillageServer System

The Raspberry Pi is the small local server. It stores the library and creates the local Wi‑Fi experience people use from their phones.

Plain answer

Think of it as a small bookshelf with Wi‑Fi. The phone connects to the bookshelf nearby instead of reaching out to the internet.

What it does

Hosts the local library, organizes content, and lets phones browse resources nearby.

What it needs

Power, storage, a prepared library, and a simple label showing the Wi‑Fi name and local address.

What users see

They join Wi‑Fi, open the library page, and choose resources to read, hear, watch, or save.

Board takeaway

This is the core device, but the value is the local access it creates for ordinary phones.
