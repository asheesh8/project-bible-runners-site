Group media

A projector turns one resource into a group moment.

Not every person will have a phone, storage, or confidence using files. Projector support lets a leader show gospel films, training clips, Scripture media, and children’s resources to a gathered group.

Prepare well

Most projector problems are setup problems.

  • Power planKnow whether the projector runs from battery, solar, or local power.
  • Input planKnow the cable or wireless adapter before the gathering starts.
  • Speaker planUse an external speaker if the group is larger than a room.
  • Backup fileKeep the same video on USB or phone if streaming fails.

Set it up

Run a village movie night in five steps.

A projector turns one resource into a shared moment. Walk the steps, then check the connection you will use and pack from the list.

  2. 2Power up
  3. 3Connect

1 · Choose the place

Find shade or darkness, a stable table, and a surface for a screen — a portable screen, white sheet, or a plain wall all work.

2 · Power up

Confirm the projector and speaker run from battery, solar, or local power before people gather. See power & solar →

3 · Connect the source

Use the VillageServer, a phone, a USB drive, or a computer as the media source. Pick the matching connection in the tabs below.

4 · Test the sound

People forgive imperfect video far faster than audio they cannot hear. Set volume and test the speaker before the group arrives.

5 · Share afterward

When the showing ends, help interested people save or receive the resource on their phones. See phone‑based distribution →

Connecting it up

Connect the projector

  • HDMI — the most common method; plug-and-play.
  • USB media — play stored content straight from a drive.
  • Wireless — stream over the local network where supported.

Connect the audio

  • Bluetooth — pair a rechargeable or solar speaker.
  • 3.5 mm — a simple cable to most speakers.
  • USB / HDMI audio — route sound through the same connection as video.
