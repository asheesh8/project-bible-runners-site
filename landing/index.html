Our mission

VillageServer places the complete Word of God — Bibles, teaching, and gospel films — into the hands of people with no internet, no power grid, and no technician, and makes sharing it simple enough that one villager can pass it to the next.

How the reach multiplies

One library. Five ways to reach further.

The same offline VillageServer library scales from a single card in one hand to a satellite signal serving a whole region. Each step multiplies how many people the Word reaches.

  1. 1

    microSD card

    Reaches 1 person

    Load the full library — Bibles, teaching, and films — onto a single card. One card, one person, fully offline.

    How sharing works →
  2. 2

    Wi-Fi sharing hub

    Up to 5 phones

    A pocket hub broadcasts the card wirelessly, so several nearby phones can browse and download the same library at once.

    Phone distribution →
  3. 3

    Raspberry Pi VillageServer

    20–40 people

    A low-power server hosts the whole library over its own Wi-Fi, serving a gathering of phones with no internet and no data.

    Field configurations →
  4. 4

    Projector & audio

    Hundreds at once

    Play gospel films and teaching to a whole village at a single showing — one screen, one crowd, one evening.

    Projector systems →
  5. 5

    Satellite receive-and-replay

    Thousands across a region

    A satellite feed refreshes and rebroadcasts the library across an entire region — reaching 150+ and scaling into the thousands.

    Satellite systems →
