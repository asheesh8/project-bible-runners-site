Power systems & solar solutions

Reliable power for every VillageServer.

Every VillageServer depends on a source of electrical power, so selecting the proper power system is one of the most important decisions in planning a deployment — especially in villages, mission stations, refugee camps, and rural regions where power may be limited or unavailable.

Why power matters

Without power, information cannot flow.

Every component of the VillageServer system requires power — Raspberry Pi units, phones and tablets, portable projectors, audio systems, satellite equipment, Wi-Fi devices, LED lighting, laptops, charging stations, and educational equipment.

Without a dependable power source, access to information, communication, and training materials becomes limited. For this reason, power planning should always be considered an essential part of any deployment.

Power options

From wall outlets to battery banks.

01

Existing electrical service

The simplest source. Where commercial power is available, equipment can run directly from homes, churches, schools, community centers, mission stations, and clinics.

02

Portable battery power

One of the most flexible methods — quiet operation, no fuel, no emissions, portable, and easy to operate.

03

Power banks

Small rechargeable battery packs for phones, tablets, and other low-power devices.

04

Portable power stations

Larger battery systems that power multiple devices at the same time.

05

Battery banks

Collections of larger batteries connected together to store greater amounts of electrical energy.

06

Generators & hybrids

Generators provide another source of power, and many successful installations combine several sources into a hybrid system.

Solar power systems

Often the most valuable source in remote places.

Solar power offers renewable energy, no fuel costs, quiet operation, low maintenance, and long-term sustainability.

☀

Portable solar panels

Lightweight panels that can be transported and deployed as needed.

☀

Solar suitcases

Foldable solar systems designed for transportation and field deployment.

☀

Fixed solar installations

For permanently installed VillageServer systems, fixed solar may be the best long-term option.

Phone charging stations

One practical ministry service is providing phone charging capability for the local community.

Power planning

Questions to answer before deploying.

  • How much power?Total power the kit will require.
  • How many devices?Number operating at the same time.
  • Is service available?Whether electrical service exists.
  • Is solar practical?Sunlight and placement at the site.
  • What backups?Available backup power systems.
  • How many days?Days of operation required.
