Board brief - stories and proof

Testimonials

Real stories from missionaries and local leaders showing what changed when gospel resources became available offline.

Plain answer

A useful testimony does not need to sound polished. It should say what changed, who was helped, and what should happen next.

Field steps

Use it this way

  1. Ask one clear questionWhat changed because the resources were available offline?
  2. Get permissionConfirm whether the name, photo, location, and quote can be shared publicly.
  3. Keep it shortUse two or three sentences that a board member can understand quickly.
  4. Tie it to actionConnect the story to phone sharing, a local library, projector teaching, or field training.
  5. Protect sensitive placesUse general location language when public details could create risk.

Good quote

Specific, short, and connected to access.

Good photo

Shows people learning, setting up, or sharing resources.

Good update

Includes what worked and what still needs support.
