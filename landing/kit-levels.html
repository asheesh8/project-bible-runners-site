Kit planning

Start with the core. Add what the field setting needs.

VillageServer should not be presented as one expensive bundle for every location. A village may need only the local server and language cards; another setting may require solar, projector, charging, and satellite support.

Deployment levels

Each level adds a clear capability.

01

Basic Kit

Raspberry Pi VillageServer plus language cards. Best for phone-based local access.

02

Media Kit

Basic Kit plus projector support for group showings and teaching.

03

Power Kit

Media Kit plus solar suitcase support for locations with unreliable electricity.

04

Community Access Kit

Power Kit plus supervised phone charging to serve practical community needs.

05

Satellite-Enabled Kit

Community Access Kit plus optional satellite hardware for updates and support.

?

Custom pilot

Some field pilots will need a different mix based on language, power, customs, shipping, or ministry partner needs.

Planning path

Use itemized planning, not a mystery donation button.

Choose the pieces the field setting actually needs: core server, language card, USB library, projector, solar power, satellite kit, or phone charging.

The goal is to help the team understand the setting before promising hardware or raising support around it.
