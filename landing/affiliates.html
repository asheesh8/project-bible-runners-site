Project Bible Runners

Field deployment partner & founding ministry

Field Ministry
▾

Project Bible Runners is the founding field ministry behind VillageServer. Founded by Jessica, Bible Runners exists to get God's Word into the hands of people who have never held a Bible — whether that means hiking into a remote village, organizing community screenings, or equipping local leaders to carry the work forward long after the team leaves.

Jessica leads the field side: building relationships in communities, coordinating deployments, and making sure the technology the VillageServer team builds is actually useful to real people in real settings. Her feedback from the field directly shapes the kit design and training materials.

What Bible Runners brings: trusted community relationships, field leadership, spiritual oversight, and the boots-on-the-ground experience that keeps VillageServer grounded in the actual mission — not just the technology.

DBS

Digital Bible Society

Scripture content libraries & digital resource network

Content Partner
▾

The Digital Bible Society provides the Scripture content that lives on every VillageServer language card. DBS has spent years building curated digital Bible libraries — text, audio, and video — in hundreds of languages, formatted specifically for offline devices.

Rather than VillageServer building its own content library from scratch, we use DBS's pre-built ZIM packages which include full Bible text, dramatised audio recordings, and the Jesus Film Project video library in the relevant language. This means a field kit is ready to use the moment it arrives — the content is already loaded, licensed, and tested.

What DBS brings: hundreds of offline-ready language libraries, established relationships with Bible translation organizations worldwide, and licensing that allows free distribution in field settings.

TS

TechSoup

Nonprofit technology discounts & hardware sourcing

Technology Partner
▾

TechSoup helps nonprofits access software, hardware, and technology services at dramatically reduced costs. For a mission-focused initiative like VillageServer, that means Raspberry Pi kits, microSD cards, solar hardware, and projector systems can be sourced at a fraction of retail price — keeping kit costs low enough that small churches and field organizations can afford them.

TechSoup provides discounts on hardware through partner manufacturers, cloud credits, software licensing, and access to a nonprofit technology community that shares sourcing knowledge globally.

What TechSoup brings: meaningful hardware cost reductions, a vetted nonprofit procurement process, and a network of technology partners that can support specific field needs as the initiative scales.
